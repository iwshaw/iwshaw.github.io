This is the Arctic region, showing Northern Canada, Greenland and the North Pole.

In the 32 years from 1984 to 2016 the Arctic lost over 94% of its older, thicker ice.

Ice that has built up over the years is mostly thicker and harder to melt than newer ice.

The age of the ice is indicated by shades ranging from blue-gray for the youngest ice to white for the oldest.
