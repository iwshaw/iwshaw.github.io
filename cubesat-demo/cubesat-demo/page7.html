The area covered by Arctic sea ice at least four years old has decreased from 718,000 square miles in September 1984 to 42,000 square miles in September 2016.

Ice that has built up over the years tends to be thicker and less vulnerable to melting away than newer ice.

In these visualizations of data from buoys, weather stations, satellites and computer models, the age of the ice is indicated by shades ranging from blue-gray for the youngest ice to white for the oldest.
