Transponders are devices that send a radio signal that satellites can detect.


Birds like Ospreys can be fitted with transponders.


By listening to the radio signals, satellites can learn where the birds are, and we can track what path the birds have taken.

This picture shows a GPS transponder 'backpack' on an Osprey at Rothiemurchus Fishery.

Ospreys are fish-eating birds and stayed close to rivers and coastlines along the journey.

We can learn a lot about the behaviour of these birds from following their seasonal movements.

All of the Ospreys went to North Africa except one called Caledonia, who stopped in Seville, Spain.

The males were faster on average, but one female, Alba, was the fastest of all.
