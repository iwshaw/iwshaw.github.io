Shanghai is the perfect example of how China’s cities have grown rapidly over the past forty years.

Its population doubled from 12 million in 1982 to 24 million in 2016.

Analysis of satellite imagery found that the city’s area grew from 119 square miles (308 square kilometers) in 1984, to 503 square miles (1,302 square kilometers) in 2014.

In these images, developed areas appear gray and white; farmland and forests are green; shallow, muddy water is tan.
