In mid-March 2017, it began to rain very heavily in Peru.

According to reports, more than 70,000 people lost their homes and more than 60 people died in floods and mudslides.

Two of the local rivers filled with so much water, they spilled over their banks into the surrounding area.

Official reports say that about 4,660 miles of roads and more than 500 bridges were damaged.
