These images come from a volcano in northern Ethiopia called Erta Ale.

Translated into “smoking mountain” with a vent called “the gateway to hell,” Erta Ale oozed a lava lake on Jan. 10, and released its trapped volcanic gasses on Feb. 11.

Other hot spots were detected on the southeastern slope of the volcano, as shown in the Feb. 11 image.

The satellite’s shortwave infrared (SWIR) signal can see increased temperatures where new lava is either very shallow, or gurgling up to the surface.
