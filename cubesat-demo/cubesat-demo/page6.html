China’s Three Gorges Dam on the Yangtze River became the world’s largest hydroelectric power plant when it was completed in 2012.

It has also reduced flooding, helped with river navigation and provided water for irrigation.

However, construction forced about 1.2 million people to move house and, according to a 2010 study, triggered about 3,400 earthquakes and many landslides from mid-2003 to 2009.

Changes in river flow have raised concerns about the buildup of mud on the river bottom, and loss of different species of wildlife.

Important archeological sites and ancient monuments reportedly ended up underwater as the reservoir filled.
